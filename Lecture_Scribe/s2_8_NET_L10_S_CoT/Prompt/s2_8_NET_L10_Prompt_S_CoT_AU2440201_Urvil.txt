1 Why You Are Doing This
In this task, you will use a Large Language Model (ChatGPT 5.2 Model) to generate
lecture scribes under a fixed prompting style assigned to your group. Each member
of the group must follow the prompting style assigned to their respective group, but you
will make the scribe individually. These scribes serve two purposes:
1. Academic reference material suitable for preparing for a reading-based / closednotes exam
2. It is the graded component of the course.
In this activity, your freedom to prompt is intentionally constrained. The goal is to
evaluate disciplined and reproducible use of AI, not creative expression.
This activity does not evaluate:
• creativity
• personal writing style
• maximum verbosity
This activity evaluates:
• precision in prompt formulation
• faithfulness in following instructions
2 What a Lecture Scribe Is (Purpose)
A lecture scribe is a faithful, exam-ready reconstruction of what was taught in
class, written so that a student can later rely on it as reference material while studying.
A correct lecture scribe should allow a student to:
• Recall definitions and notation
• Reconstruct proofs and derivations
• Follow example solutions step by step
• Understand logical dependencies between concepts
A lecture scribe should answer the question:
“If I study only this document, can I reliably revise what was taught?”
2.1 What This Implies for Content
The scribe should include:
• Definitions and notation
• Assumptions and conditions
1
• Statements of theorems or results
• Proofs or proof sketches, step by step
• Worked examples, with intermediate steps
• Clear logical flow between ideas
2.2 What a Lecture Scribe Is NOT
A lecture scribe is not:
• A creative explanation
• A simplified tutorial
• A textbook rewrite
• A solution manual for unseen problems
• A place to add intuition not discussed in class
Everything included must be traceable to the provided context.
3 Context Rules (Applies to All Prompting Styles)
3.1 What “Context” Means
The context is the authoritative source material the model is allowed to use. For this
activity, the context is fixed and common for all:
• Lecture slides/lecture PDF
• Textbook content which is relevant to the respective lecture.
You must attach only these materials, period.
3.2 What Is Allowed in the Context
• Lecture slides or PDFs
• Textbook content which is relevant to the respective lecture.
• Proofs, derivations, and examples that appear in those materials
3.3 What Is Not Allowed in the Context
• Solved material not shown or discussed in lecture
• Instructor annotations explaining how to write a scribe
• Reference scribes or “ideal answers”
• Writing guides disguised as content
Important clarification: An example scribe is allowed only inside the prompt for
one-shot prompting. Example scribes are never part of the context for the rest.
4 What Every Prompt Must Communicate (All Styles)
Regardless of prompting style, your prompt must clearly communicate that:
• The output is a lecture scribe
• The scribe is intended as exam reference material
2
• The model must:
– prioritize correctness
– include explicit reasoning when appropriate
– follow the lecture’s structure and scope
• The model must not invent content
This is a purpose constraint, not a template.
5 Global Rules About Prompting Styles
1. Your group is assigned one prompting style for the entire semester.
2. You may refine how you use that style, but you may not switch styles.
3. You may not borrow elements from other prompting styles.
4. Only your prompt text is evaluated for improvement.
